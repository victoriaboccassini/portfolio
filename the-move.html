KEY Insights

Users cannot efficiently compare multiple apartment listings

The platform displays one listing at a time, forcing users to open multiple tabs and rely on memory to compare 3-5 finalist apartments. This memory-dependent task increases cognitive load and can lead to decision fatigue or choosing based on incomplete information.

Evidence: • 6/8 users in usability testing opened 3+ browser tabs simultaneously, each showing a different apartment listing
• Analytics data shows 45% of sessions involve users having 3+ apartment listings open at the same time.
"I made a spreadsheet with all the details because I couldn't keep track"
→ Build side-by-side comparison feature for up to 3 apartments showing key attributes in unified table format.

Technical Debt Erodes Trust at Critical Moments

Users experience technical friction (slow uploads, buggy chat) at the highest-commitment moments (document submission, contacting landlords). In a marketplace built on trust for high-stakes transactions, technical glitches signal unreliability and may cause users to question if the platform can be trusted.

Evidence: • In-house document upload and chat systems have capacity/performance issues.
• Engineering reports scalability constraints.
→ Replace in-house solutions DocuSign (documents) and Salesforce/Intercom (communication) to improve reliability.

Manual Processes Create Bi-Directional Anxiety

In a competitive rental market, manual processes create delays that generate anxiety in tenants (fear of losing apartment) and frustration in landlords (repetitive work). This bi-directional friction damages satisfaction on both sides and positions The Move as a slow intermediary rather than a value-adding facilitator.

Evidence: • Document verification takes 24-48hrs (manual review).
• 5/8 tenants reported anxiety about "losing the apartment" during delays.
• Landlords frustrated by repetitive questions.
→ Automate verification with instant background checks API, add instant confirmation messages, implement FAQ bot, and add application status tracking.

Natural Language Gap Between Search Intent and Tools

There's a fundamental mismatch between how tenants conceptualize their ideal living space (lifestyle, feeling, experience) and how the platform enables search (rigid, technical). This forces users to translate their desires into filters that can't capture nuance, resulting in missed matches and excessive browsing time.

Evidence: • Tenants describe ideal apartments experientially ("bright, open space with nature views") but platform offers only technical filters (bedrooms, sqft, checkboxes).
"I want a flat with big windows"
→ Implement "The Move +" AI-powered natural language search for tenants + auto feature extraction from photos for landlords.

The Move - AI powered proposal

Tenant experience

In our pursuit of enhancing the tenant experience, we recognize the tremendous potential of harnessing artificial intelligence. By implementing advanced techniques like data augmentation and feature extraction, we can revolutionize the way users search for their ideal rental properties.

The innovative approach allows tenants to describe their ideal apartment without being confined by traditional classifications. This approach empowers tenants to express their preferences in their own words, leading to more nuanced and tailored search results. By utilizing AI algorithms, we can analyze these descriptions, extract relevant features, and skyrocket the likelihood of matching users with their desired properties.

Landlord experience
